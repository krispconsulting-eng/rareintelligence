Brand Guidelines · v1.0

The Deep Signal identity.

Rare by nature. Intelligent by design.

A disciplined cool-blue system — midnight navy at the base, lifting through indigo and cobalt into ocean and a single vivid teal. No warm hues anywhere; the absence is the point. Restraint is what reads as “rare”.

01 · At a glance

Three principles decide every call.

🧊

Restraint is the brand

A cool palette with no warm hues. The discipline — the absence — is what reads as rare. Never reach outside Deep Signal.

⚡

Teal earns its glow

Vivid teal is the single brightest note. One primary action or highlight per view — spend it like punctuation.

🦓

Texture is felt, not seen

The zebra — the universal symbol of rare disease — sits beneath everything at low opacity. Present, never dominant.

05 · Voice & tone

A trusted colleague — not a guru.

Direct, specific, grounded, warm, anti-hype. Speaks from lived experience and doesn’t need to announce itself.

Always

  • Concrete, calm, credible
  • Specific tools & workflows
  • Plain, peer-to-peer language
  • Time saved, named outcomes

Never

  • Transformative, revolutionary
  • Game-changing, leverage synergies
  • Guru or corporate-clinical tone
  • Vague hype & abstraction

On-brand: “This workflow drafts your grant report in twenty minutes — here’s exactly how.”

06 · Texture & do / don’t

The zebra whispers.

A fine 35° diagonal stripe rides beneath surfaces at 10–20% opacity. Felt, not seen — never above 30%.

Do

  • Keep navy / white as the primary background
  • Spend teal as a single accent per view
  • Use the gradient on bands & headers only
  • Reserve Lora italic for taglines & quotes
  • Let the zebra whisper at 10–20%
  • Write peer-to-peer, concrete, calm

Don’t

  • Fill large areas with vivid teal
  • Stack teal + cobalt + indigo to compete
  • Use the gradient as full-page wallpaper
  • Set body copy in Lora or DM Mono
  • Push texture loud or use literal zebras
  • Reach for hype or guru language
